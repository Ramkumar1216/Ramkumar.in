## 5. TONE & VOICE GUIDELINES

### Tone:
```
Professional but conversational
Confident but not arrogant
Results-focused but empathetic
Direct but friendly
```

### Examples:

❌ **WEAK**: "I utilize advanced analytics techniques to optimize your business metrics."
✅ **STRONG**: "I show you which customers make money and which lose it. Then we double down on what works."

❌ **WEAK**: "My passion is helping businesses grow."
✅ **STRONG**: "When you align your business around data, revenue becomes a choice, not luck."

❌ **WEAK**: "I'm an innovative digital strategist."
✅ **STRONG**: "I help eCommerce sellers reduce their cost per acquisition by 40% in 60 days."

## 10. OBJECTION-HANDLING COPY

### Objection: "I don't have a budget"
**Response**: 
```
"I work with businesses of all sizes. Let's talk about what's possible at your budget.
Many clients start with one service, see ROI, then expand. That's the smart way."
```

### Objection: "I don't have time for this"
**Response**:
```
"Most of this work I do. Your time = 5-6 hours total across the entire project.
Everything else is on me. Clear?"

### Objection: "Will this actually work for MY business?"
**Response**:
```
"I won't know until I see your data and understand your situation.
That's exactly why the free 20-minute call exists.
Let's look at YOUR numbers and I'll show you what's possible."

## 12. DAILY POSITIONING REMINDERS

Before ANY customer communication, ask:

✅ Did I focus on THEIR benefit or MY skills?
✅ Does it show measurable results or vague promises?
✅ Would they feel "I need this person" after reading?
✅ Is the tone professional but conversational?
✅ Did I avoid generic words?
✅ Is there a clear next step?
✅ Does it start with their problem?
